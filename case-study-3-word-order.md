# Case Study 3: Word Order & Translation Influence

## Test Goal
Evaluate whether the sentence follows natural Chinese word order and avoids direct translation from English structures.

## Original Sentence
我每天都做我的作业在晚上。

## Issue Identified
- Word order is unnatural due to English influence (“在晚上” placed at the end)
- “做我的作业” reflects literal translation from English (“do my homework”)
- Overall sentence structure does not align with native Chinese expression patterns

## Improved Version
我每天晚上都做作业。

## Analysis
The original sentence demonstrates strong influence from English sentence structure, particularly in word order. In Chinese, time expressions such as “每天晚上” are typically placed earlier in the sentence, rather than at the end.

Additionally, the phrase “做我的作业” is a direct translation from English. In natural Chinese, possessive pronouns are often omitted in this context, and “做作业” is the standard expression.

This example highlights a common issue in AI-generated or learner Chinese: **word order transfer and literal translation**, which result in grammatically understandable but unnatural sentences.

Improving word order and reducing unnecessary translation artifacts significantly enhances fluency and aligns the sentence with native-level expression.
